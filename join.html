RECRUITMENT
  1. APPLICATION - We conduct recruitment twice a year, at the beginning of every semester. Returning members do not have to fill out the written application but will be required to interview. The Fall 2017 application can be accessed here and is due on September 1st. We have mandatory club meetings every Friday 5-6PM, excluding academic breaks. Please only apply to VCG if you are available at this time.
  2. INTERVIEW - If selected to interview, you will meet with members of the executive board to answer behavioral questions, to elaborate on your interest in joining VCG, and to conduct a basic case interview. There are two short case questions: one on profit optimization and one on market sizing. Interviews typically last between 10-15 minutes. Interviews will take place on September 2nd and September 3rd at the Starbucks on the Corner. 
  3. MEMBERSHIP INVITATION - The weekend after interviews complete, a final decision is delivered to your email address. We either extend a membership invitation, a waitlist notification, or advice on how to improve your application for the next recruitment cycle. Potential members typically have 2 days to respond to our offer before we extend invitations to our waitlist candidates.

Initial Training and Team Meeting

Once accepted into our organization, we have an initial meeting where you will learn about your expectations as an analyst, receive basic training on client interaction, and meet your team for the first time.

Workshops and Work Sessions

Every other week, the organization hosts educational workshops ranging from data visualization to best case interview practices. We bring in outside organizations, such as Bain, to host these workshops alongside our executive board. 

We host organization-wide workshops every Friday from 5-6PM. Analysts are expected to attend and work with their team to discuss the week's findings and plans moving forward. The entire executive board will be available to answer any questions that may arise.

Client Meetings

Teams typically meet with their client face-to-face or over phone call weekly. Members discuss the week's progress and receive work assignments for the future. Project leaders faciliate client interaction and are responsible for meeting the team's deadlines.

Organization Mixers

Get to meet your fellow analysts in a casual setting. Previous social events have included renting out all of JumpCville (yes, we had the entire place to ourselves!), enjoying smoothies together, and relaxing at pre-games. 

Final Presentation and Deliverable

At the end of the semester, all teams will present their findings to other analysts, the executive board, and clients. Teams are also expected to prepare a deliverable summarizing the semester's work and recommendations for the client.

FAQ

What is consulting?

Google defines consulting as the business of giving expert advice to other professionals, typically in financial and business matters.

We define consulting as understanding a client's problems, working successfully with a team to solve these problems, and having a great time doing so.

 How is VCG different from other consulting groups?

Our organization provides pro-bono consulting to both non-profit and for-profit firms. We want our members to be exposed to as many different types of organizations as possible, since this accurately reflects how broad the field of consulting is. We don't narrow our clients to a specific field and also strive to have weekly productive client meetings, something rare among other consulting groups.

 What is your acceptance rate?

Our acceptance rate varies every year. We generally accept around 25-30% of our applicants as analysts, with interviews being extended to 40-50% of the initial applicant pool.
